-CURRICULUM-

Play-Group

The curriculum for the Playgroup class is designed to provide a stimulating and nurturing environment for children to develop their social, emotional, physical, and cognitive skills. The focus is on fostering a love for learning through play-based activities, exploration, and interactive experiences. The curriculum encourages children to develop their communication and language skills, enhance their creativity, and promote overall holistic development.
Assessment:
Assessment in the Playgroup class will be primarily based on observation and informal methods. Teachers will monitor and evaluate children's progress based on their active participation, engagement, and developmental milestones achieved. Regular parent-teacher meetings will be conducted to discuss the child's progress and provide feedback.

Nursery

The curriculum for the Nursery class is designed to provide a nurturing and engaging learning environment for children to develop their social, emotional, physical, and cognitive skills. The focus is on promoting holistic development through play-based activities, exploration, and interactive experiences. The curriculum aims to enhance children's language and communication skills, numeracy skills, creativity, and overall readiness for formal schooling.
Assessment in the Nursery class will be primarily based on observation, ongoing assessment, and informal methods. Regular parent-teacher meetings will be conducted to discuss the child's progress and provide feedback.

Lower Kindergarten

-LKG-

The curriculum for the LKG class is designed to provide a comprehensive and developmentally appropriate learning experience for children. The focus is on promoting social, emotional, cognitive, and physical development through a well-rounded curriculum that includes language development, numeracy skills, creativity, and overall readiness for formal schooling.
Assessment in the LKG class will be based on ongoing observation, continuous evaluation, and informal methods. Teachers will assess children's progress through class participation, performance in activities, and developmental milestones achieved. Parent-teacher meetings will be conducted regularly to discuss the child's progress and provide feedback.

Upper Kindergarten

-UKG-

The curriculum for the UKG class is designed to provide a comprehensive and structured learning experience for children. The focus is on fostering the development of essential skills and knowledge required for a smooth transition to formal schooling. The curriculum emphasizes language development, numeracy skills, critical thinking, creativity, and overall readiness for primary education.
